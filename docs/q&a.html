Q&A Section:

Most Frequently asked questions:


What can i do to better care for my dogs coat?:

  • Wire-haired: Benefit from professional grooming a few times a year.
  • Smooth-haired: Minimal grooming needed — brushing is usually enough.
  • Long-haired: Occasional trimming around paws, ears, and tail helps.

Quick tip dachshunds tend to be prone to skin issues due to their coats so brushing can help to spot any skin issues early.


How often should i bathe my dachshund?:

Bathing is only needed when its neccessary a duration of once every 1-2 months or whenever dirty (ensure that a shampoo is specifically for your dogs type of hair, this avoids drying out the skin or causing further harm to their coat).


What should i feed my dachshund?:

Balancing out the diet with omega-3 and omega 6 fatty acids can help to support a healthier skin and shiny coat.


Do i need to take my dachshund to the groomers?:

Wire haired dachshunds benefit the most from grooming a couple times a year but not too often as it helps to keep them warm during the colder times of the year. Smooth haired dachshunds really require grooming and in turn only need to be brushed Long haired dachshunds can be taken to the groomers as well but the main areas to focus on trimmming are the paws, ears and tail.


How often should my dachshund exercise?:

-Dachshunds need at most around 45minutes to an hour per day but it can vary depending on the amount of playtime they have at home. Make sure to be wary of any high intensive movement as it can potentially affect their back.


How prone are dachshunds to separation anxiety

Dachshunds are able to bond very closely to their owners making it hard for them to to be separated for long durations of time. With proper training they can cope better.

Training tips for your dachshund

    Starting early:

  • Starting early makes it significantly easier to train a puppy as they respond to commands more quickly and aren't as temperamental as older dogs

    • Keep it short and simple:

  • Around 5 to 10 minutes a day is more than enough to help train a dachshund puppy as they can become distracted or frustrated.

    • Be positive:

  • Any form of positive reinforcement can help a young dachshund puppy to better understand the clear difference between good or bad actions

    • Be consistent

  • Ensuring that the training on hand follows through whether that training occurs once a day or two times a week can help build a routine for the dog and owner ensuring that a clear bonding experience occurs making it more efficient for both parties.
