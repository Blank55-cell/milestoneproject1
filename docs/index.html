Smooth Coat:

Smooth coated Dachshunds have short, shiny fur that lies close to the body. This is the most common coat type, requiring minimal grooming but offering little insulation in cold weather. Negatives: They can feel the cold easily, may need sweaters in winter, and their sleek coat shows scratches or skin issues more visibly.

Long Haired Coat:

Long haired Dachshunds feature soft, flowing fur with feathering on the ears, chest, and legs. They need regular brushing to prevent tangles and are known for their elegant appearance. Negatives: Their coat mats quickly if not maintained, they shed more, and grooming takes extra time and effort.

Wire Haired Coat:

Wire haired Dachshunds have a dense, rough outer coat with bushy eyebrows and a beard. Their scruffy look adds charm, but they require hand stripping or trimming to maintain coat health. Negatives: Grooming can be more specialized, making them feel uncomfortable and anxious due to the high volume of dogs that groomers get. It may be quite costly, their coat feels rougher to touch, and they may be prone to itchiness if not properly maintained.


History of Dachshunds

Dachshunds originated in Germany over 300 years ago. They were bred to hunt small game such as badgers (their name literally translates to badger dog), which is the main reason why they love burrowing and have long bodies perfect for making their way through burrows. They feature strong lungs and strong paws allowing them to excavate and dig through layers of soil and dirt.


Fun Facts

  • Dachshunds were the first official Olympic mascot (Waldi, Munich 1972)
  • Famous owners include: Picasso, Danny DeVito, John Wayne and Andy Warhol.
  • Dachshunds have a very loud bark especially for their size which makes them incredibly good watchdogs
  • They tend to have a very stubborn nature and can be hard to train, but if you want tips on how to get started you can click here: Q&A
  • Dachshunds tend to live between 12 and 16 years. The oldest dachshund recorded was Rocky, a smooth haired dachshund from Shingle Springs, California, who lived to be 25 years old.
