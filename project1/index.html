Smooth Coat:

Smooth-coated Dachshunds have short, shiny fur that lies close to the body. This is the most common coat type, requiring minimal grooming but offering little insulation in cold weather. Negatives: They can feel the cold easily, may need sweaters in winter, and their sleek coat shows scratches or skin issues more visibly.

Long Haired Coat:


Long-haired Dachshunds feature soft, flowing fur with feathering on the ears, chest, and legs. They need regular brushing to prevent tangles and are known for their elegant appearance. Negatives: Their coat mats quickly if not maintained, they shed more, and grooming takes extra time and effort.

Wire Haired Coat:


Wire-haired Dachshunds have a dense, rough outer coat with bushy eyebrows and a beard. Their scruffy look adds charm, but they require hand-stripping or trimming to maintain coat health. Negatives: Grooming can be more specialized making them feel more uncomfortable and anxious due to the high volume of dpgs that groomers get it may be quite costly, their coat feels rougher to touch, and they may be prone to itchiness if not properly maintained.


History of Dachshunds

Dachshunds originated in Germany over 300 years ago. They were bred to hunt small game such as badgers (their name literally translates to badger dog) which is the main reason why they love burrowing and have long bodies perfect for being able to make their way through long burrows. They feature strong lungs and srong paws allowing them to excavate and dig through layers of soil and dirt.


Fun Facts

  • Famous owners include: Picasso, Danny Devito, John Wayne and Andy Warhol.
  • Dachshunds have a very loud bark especially for their size which makes them incredibly good watchdogs
  • They tend to have a very stubborn nature and tend to be quite hard to train but if you want to know some tips on how to get started on training one you can click here Q&A
  • Dachshunds tend to live between the ages of 12 to around 16 years with the oldest dachshund to have ever lived being a smooth haired dachshund by the name of Rocky from Shingle Springs, California who lived to be 25 years old.
